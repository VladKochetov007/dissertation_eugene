\chapter{Policy Methods in Reinforcement Learning}
\label{ch:policy-methods}

This chapter introduces the mathematical framework of reinforcement learning that underpins the rest of the dissertation. We begin with the Markov Decision Process, which formalises how an agent interacts with an uncertain environment, and then develop the key objects---policies, value functions, and returns---needed to state optimality conditions. In the second half of the chapter we turn to \emph{policy gradient methods}, a family of algorithms that directly parametrise and optimise the agent's policy. The material in this chapter follows \cite{sutton2018rl}, primarily Chapters~3 and~13; all notation introduced here will be extended to the multi-agent setting in later chapters.

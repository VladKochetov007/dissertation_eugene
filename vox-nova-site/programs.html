Our Programs

From sprint to fellowship

A research pipeline designed for speed, rigor, and global access. No credentials required — just demonstrated ability.

Research Sprints

48 hours. One question. Hundreds of researchers.

Our sprints are intensive hybrid hackathons where participants from around the world tackle a focused research question about progress in the developing world. Teams form on Friday evening and present findings by Sunday afternoon.

No PhD required. No institutional affiliation needed. If you can analyze data, write clearly, or bring domain expertise — you belong here.

How It Works

Friday Evening

Kick-off presentation, problem framing, team formation. Mentors introduce the question and available datasets.

Saturday

Research and build. Work with your team, consult mentors on call, iterate on your analysis or policy brief.

Sunday by 4pm

Submit your project. Evening presentations and judging by expert panel.

Monday

Winners announced. Top teams invited to the Studio program. Best outputs published on our site.

Frequency6-8 per year
Duration48 hours
FormatOnline + Hub Cities
Participants200+ per sprint
PrizesCash + Studio invite
CostFree

Studio

4 weeks to turn a good idea into real research.

The Studio is a part-time research accelerator for top sprint performers. You'll refine your research question, learn rigorous methodology, get feedback from domain experts, and produce a working paper or research proposal.

Think of it as a research bootcamp — structured enough to push you forward, flexible enough to work alongside a job or studies.

The 4-Week Arc

Week 1: Question

Research question refinement, literature review, identifying your contribution to existing knowledge.

Week 2: Method

Methodology workshop, data identification, causal reasoning training. What evidence would answer your question?

Week 3: Analysis

Crunch the data. Write the draft. Get feedback from your mentor and cohort peers.

Week 4: Present

Present to an expert panel. Receive structured feedback. Best projects advance to Fellowship consideration.

Frequency3-4 cohorts/year
Duration4 weeks part-time
Commitment10-15 hrs/week
Cohort Size15-25
SelectionFrom sprint outputs
CostFree

Research Fellowship

Funded, mentored, and publication-track.

Our flagship program. Fellows spend 3-6 months producing peer-reviewed research on progress in the developing world. You get a stipend calibrated to your local cost of living, compute resources, 1:1 mentorship from a senior researcher, and publication support.

We've designed this for researchers who are building careers in this space — not just writing one paper and moving on.

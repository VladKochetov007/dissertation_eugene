Research Agenda

Five pillars of inquiry

We don't prescribe research conclusions. We frame the questions that matter most for progress in the developing world.

01

Institutions & Governance

Why do some countries build effective institutions while others don't? What makes institutional reform stick?

Key Questions

Post-conflict institution building: what determines success vs. state fragility?
Anti-corruption mechanisms that actually reduce corruption, not just measure it
Decentralization: when does devolving power improve outcomes?
Digital governance adoption: e-Estonia model portability to other contexts
New economic jurisdictions, charter cities, and special economic zones
Judicial independence and the rule of law as growth prerequisites

Case Studies We're Exploring

Rwanda's state capacity Georgia's anti-corruption reform Botswana's institutional outlier status Baltic digital governance Colombia's peace process Singapore's development state
02

Technology & Industrial Development

How do countries move up the value chain? When does industrial policy work — and when is it rent-seeking?

Key Questions

Special economic zones: success conditions vs. white elephants
Technology transfer mechanisms and absorptive capacity
Local innovation ecosystems: what makes a tech hub emerge?
Leapfrogging vs. sequential development paths
Diaspora entrepreneurs as technology bridges
AI adoption in low-infrastructure environments

Case Studies We're Exploring

Vietnam's manufacturing ascent India's IT services boom Kenya's M-Pesa ecosystem Bangladesh garment industry Ethiopia's industrial parks Chile's salmon industry
03

Human Capital & Demographics

What builds productive human capital at scale? How do education, health, and migration shape long-run growth?

Key Questions

Education reform that works: what do Chile, Rwanda, and Vietnam have in common?
Brain drain or brain circulation? Measuring diaspora effects
The demographic dividend: when does a young population drive growth?
Skills mismatches and the future of work in developing economies
The role of universities as engines of local development

Case Studies We're Exploring

South Korea's education miracle India's IIT pipeline Rwanda's health system Poland's post-EU brain return Philippines' nurse export model
04

Finance & Infrastructure

How do countries fund development? What infrastructure investments have the highest returns?

Key Questions

Mobile money and financial inclusion: beyond M-Pesa
Infrastructure prioritization under constrained budgets
Debt sustainability and the new lending landscape
Remittances as development finance: magnitude and mechanisms
Crypto, stablecoins, and fintech in dollarized or unstable economies

Case Studies We're Exploring

India's UPI system Kenya's M-Pesa Indonesia's infrastructure push El Salvador's Bitcoin experiment Morocco's renewable energy
05

Culture, Ideas & Political Economy

Why do some societies embrace change while others resist? How do reform coalitions form?

Key Questions

Cultural attitudes toward entrepreneurship, risk, and innovation
How reform coalitions form and sustain themselves
Media ecosystems and information quality in developing democracies
The role of diaspora communities as bridges and catalysts
State narratives vs. bottom-up progress: when do they align?

Case Studies We're Exploring

UAE's modernization narrative Nollywood as cultural industry Taiwan's civic tech movement Georgian reform coalition Nigerian diaspora entrepreneurship

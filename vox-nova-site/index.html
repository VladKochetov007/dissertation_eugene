The Gap

Progress studies has a geography problem

The progress studies movement asks a powerful question: why does progress happen, and how do we make it happen faster?

But almost all its institutional energy is concentrated on the rich world. The conferences are in Berkeley. The policy work targets the US government. The implicit subject of "progress" is the American frontier.

Meanwhile, the places where progress is most urgent — and where the most interesting natural experiments are happening — have no equivalent research infrastructure.

We're building it.
